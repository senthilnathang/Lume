# Lume vs Notion: Which is Right for Your Team?

Both Notion and Lume are database-first platforms that let teams organize information. But they solve different problems:

- **Notion**: General knowledge management, docs, wikis, lightweight databases
- **Lume**: Production CRM systems, custom data apps, team workflows

This guide compares them across key dimensions.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Lume | Notion |
|---------|------|--------|
| **Database Scaling** | Unlimited (your DB) | Limited to Notion's infrastructure |
| **Custom Fields** | 50+ types | ~15 types |
| **Automations** | 100+ actions + webhooks | Limited integrations |
| **API Reliability** | Production-grade | Inconsistent |
| **Team Roles** | Fine-grained (record-level) | Workspace-level only |
| **Pricing** | Free (self-hosted) | $10-20/user/month |
| **Suitable For** | CRM, case management, workflows | Wikis, project planning, notes |

## When to Use Notion

Notion excels at **knowledge management**:
- Wikis and team documentation
- Light project planning
- Quick databases for small teams (<5 people)
- Personal productivity and note-taking
- Team onboarding materials

Notion's strength is **simplicity**—you can set up a database in minutes without technical knowledge.

## When to Use Lume

Lume is built for **production systems**:
- CRM and sales pipeline management
- Healthcare/legal case management
- Inventory and supply chain systems
- Custom business applications
- Data that requires compliance (HIPAA, GDPR)

Lume's strength is **flexibility and scale**—build anything, customize everything, own your data.

## The Core Difference: Databases vs. Wikis

**Notion is a wiki that has databases** (secondary feature).
**Lume is a database platform** (primary feature).

This matters for production use. Notion's database features are limited by design—they're meant to supplement documentation, not power entire systems.

## Pricing at Scale

**Notion costs scale per-user:**
- Team of 5: $50-100/month
- Team of 20: $200-400/month
- Team of 100: $1,000-2,000/month

**Lume costs don't:**
- Team of 5-100: $50-200/month (hosting only)
- Unlimited team members, bases, customization

For teams larger than 10 people, Lume is 50-80% cheaper.

## Customization & Automation

**Notion limitations:**
- Pre-defined layouts (database views, timelines, galleries)
- Integrations via Zapier (additional cost per workflow)
- No custom field types
- Slow API for bulk operations

**Lume advantages:**
- 100+ built-in automation actions
- Webhook triggers (send/receive events)
- Custom field types (healthcare, legal, scientific)
- Direct REST API with no rate limits
- Visual page builder for custom apps

## The Verdict

**Use Notion if:**
- You need team collaboration on docs and wikis
- You're tracking lightweight projects
- Your team is non-technical
- You want zero setup/maintenance

**Use Lume if:**
- You're building a CRM or production database
- You need unlimited customization
- Your team needs advanced automation
- You want to own and control your data
- You're scaling beyond 20 team members

**Use both if:**
- Use Notion for team wikis and documentation
- Use Lume for production CRM and data systems
- Integrate them via Lume's API

Many teams do exactly this—Notion for internal knowledge, Lume for customer/business data.
